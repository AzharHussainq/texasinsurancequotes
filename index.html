Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about auto insurance in Texas

What is the minimum auto insurance requirement in Texas? +

Texas requires drivers to carry at least 30/60/25 coverage: $30,000 for bodily injury per person, $60,000 for bodily injury per accident, and $25,000 for property damage. However, many experts recommend higher limits for better protection.

How can I get the cheapest auto insurance in Texas? +

To find the cheapest auto insurance in Texas, compare quotes from multiple providers, maintain a clean driving record, consider raising your deductible, ask about discounts (safe driver, multi-policy, good student), and review your coverage annually.

What factors affect my Texas auto insurance rates? +

Texas auto insurance rates are influenced by your driving record, age, location, credit history, type of vehicle, annual mileage, coverage limits, and deductibles. Urban areas like Houston and Dallas typically have higher rates than rural areas.

Is Texas a no-fault state for auto insurance? +

No, Texas is an at-fault state. This means the driver who causes an accident is responsible for covering the damages. Texas drivers can sue the at-fault driver for expenses beyond what insurance covers.
